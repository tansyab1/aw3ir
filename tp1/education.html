The Master's degree of engineering and innovation in images and networks

University Paris 13

University of Paris 13 (French: Université Paris XIII or simply Paris XIII) is one of the thirteen universities in Paris which replaced the University of Paris in 1970. It is also identified as University of Paris North (Université Paris Nord). In 2019, it was decided to change the university's name to Université Sorbonne Paris Nord.

The Master's degree "engineering and innovation in images and networks" (M3IR) aims to provide training in image processing and computer networks, professional purpose or research, along two paths (images, networks ). The first year of this master (M1), common to both courses, aims to give students the scientific basis of expertise in signal processing and image, computer networks, Java technology, and telecommunications digital. The second year (M2) offers preparatory courses for professional integration. It retains an important common core and offers a specialization via its two courses: the course "images" is moving towards the field of processing and transporting audio-visual content; the "networks" journey is focused on network technologies and the development of Internet applications. The aim is to provide students with the theoretical basis and practical mastery of multimedia and computer network technologies, in order to train future professionals destined for the industrial market of the transport and processing of audio-visual information. multimedia. This master also prepares for a doctoral thesis in the perspective of a career of researchers or teacher-researchers.
